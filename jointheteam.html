Flight Attendant

Job Description

Before the airplane takes off, you will greet passengers, secure overhead bins, and assist in the safety presentation. During the flight, you will distribute foods and beverages and respond to call lights to make sure passengers have the best possible experience. As a flight attendant, your primary responsibility is to ensure the safety of the passengers and the crew members. Throughout the flight, you should check the cockpit and the cabin to make sure they are secure. In the case of an emergency, you will have to respond quickly and carry out emergency procedures to protect the people on the flight. You will be expected to maintain a professional image. You may have to stand for extended periods of time with few breaks. Once you are hired, you will undergo a six week training program to learn about company operations, flight regulations, and job duties. You will also be provided with the full description of flight attendant responsibilities when you are hired.

Requirements

In order to be considered, you must:

  • Be at least 21 years of age at the time of application
  • Be eligible to work in the U.S.
  • Have flexible hours
  • Be prepared for physically demanding tasks if the need arises
  • Take and pass a Department of Transportation Drug Test
  • Comply with uniform guidelines
  • Preferably speak more than one language

Pilot

Job Description

During flights, there will be two pilots operating in the cockpit: the captain and the co-pilot or first officer. Prior to each flight, you will choose a flight route that is both safe and efficient, inspect the airplane, check the weather conditions, communicate with air traffic controllers, etc. During flights, you will steer the airplane, communicate with air route control stations, monitor instruments, etc. After landing, you will inspect the airplane again. Your main priority as a pilot is to ensure the safety of the flight crew and the passengers. As a pilot, you may have to work at odd hours of the day. You also may have to spend extended periods of time away from your home, staying in hotels.

Requirements

In order to be considered, you must:

  • Be at least 21 years of age at the time of application
  • Be eligible to work in the U.S.
  • Hold an Air Transport Pilot (ATP) certificate
  • Have (at least) a second class medical certificate
  • Take and pass a Department of Transportation Drug Test
  • Comply with uniform guidelines
  • Have previous flight experience
